Singapore's Best Attractions

Singapore has many amazing places to visit! From tall buildings to beautiful gardens, from fun theme parks to cultural neighborhoods. This page shows you all the top attractions you can explore during your trip.

Whether you like nature, shopping, food, or adventure - Singapore has it all! Check out our list below to plan your perfect day.

Marina Bay Sands

Location: 10 Bayfront Avenue, Singapore
Price: $25 for observation deck
Type: Landmark
Click here to learn more

Marina Bay Sands is the most famous building in Singapore! It has three tall towers with a big boat-shaped platform on top. The rooftop has a swimming pool (for hotel guests only) and an observation deck where you can see the whole city.

Inside you will find a huge shopping mall, casino, restaurants, and the ArtScience Museum. Visit in the evening to see the light and water show at the waterfront!

Gardens by the Bay

Location: 18 Marina Gardens Drive
Price: Free outdoor, $28 for domes
Type: Nature Park
Click here to learn more

Gardens by the Bay is a huge park with amazing plants and flowers. The most famous part is the Supertree Grove - giant tree structures that light up at night with music! The light show is FREE and happens at 7: 45 PM and 8:45 PM every night.

There are also two big domes: the Flower Dome and Cloud Forest. The Cloud Forest has a giant indoor waterfall! Remember to bring a jacket because the domes are very cold inside.

Sentosa Island

Location: Sentosa Island (take monorail or cable car)
Price: Varies by attraction
Type: Theme Park Island
Click here to learn more

Sentosa is a fun island with beaches, theme parks, and attractions! You can visit Universal Studios Singapore, S.E.A. Aquarium with big sharks, beautiful beaches, and adventure activities like ziplines and luge rides.

Plan to spend a whole day here because there is so much to do! The beaches are FREE to visit. You can walk to Sentosa or take the monorail from Harbourfront Station.

Merlion Park

Location: One Fullerton, Marina Bay
Price: FREE!
Type: Landmark
Click here to learn more

The Merlion is Singapore's most famous symbol! It's a statue that is half lion and half fish, and it spits water into the bay. This is the #1 place to take photos in Singapore!

The park is open 24 hours and completely free. Visit at sunset or evening for the best photos when the buildings light up. You can see Marina Bay Sands from here too!

Singapore Zoo

Location: 80 Mandai Lake Road
Price: $35 for adults
Type: Wildlife Park
Click here to learn more

Singapore Zoo is one of the best zoos in the world! Animals live in open spaces that look like their natural homes. You can see tigers, elephants, orangutans, and many more animals.

The zoo offers feeding sessions and animal shows. There is also a Night Safari next door where you can see nocturnal animals at night! Bring sunscreen and water - it's very hot!

Chinatown

Location: Chinatown District
Price: Free to walk around
Type: Cultural Area
Click here to learn more

Chinatown is a colorful neighborhood with traditional shophouses, temples, and markets. You can shop for souvenirs, try delicious Chinese food, and visit beautiful temples.

Don't miss the Chinatown Food Centre where you can try chicken rice, noodles, and desserts! Take the MRT to Chinatown Station. It's free to explore and very Instagram-worthy!
