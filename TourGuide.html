Singapore Travel Guide

Planning your trip to Singapore? Here are some helpful tips and guides to make your visit amazing! Learn about the best times to visit, how to get around, and money-saving tips.

Singapore is easy to explore, safe, and very tourist-friendly. Most people speak English and the public transport is excellent!

Getting Around Singapore

Best Way to Travel: MRT (Mass Rapid Transit)


The MRT is Singapore's train system. It's fast, cheap, and air-conditioned! You can go almost anywhere in Singapore using the MRT. Trains run from 5:30 AM to midnight.


Buy an EZ-Link card or Singapore Tourist Pass at any MRT station. The Tourist Pass gives you unlimited rides for 1, 2, or 3 days! Regular fares cost $1-2 per trip.


Buses are also good but more confusing. Taxis and Grab (like Uber) are available but more expensive. Walking is nice too — Singapore is very safe!

Best Time to Visit

Weather: Hot and humid all year!


Singapore has tropical weather. It's always hot (25-32°C / 77-90°F) and humid. There is no winter or summer — just wet season and dry season.


Dry Season (February to April): Less rain, good for outdoor activities. This is peak tourist season so it's more crowded.


Wet Season (November to January): More rain but usually just short showers. Cheaper hotels and fewer tourists! Bring an umbrella.


Pro tip: Always bring sunscreen, water, and an umbrella no matter when you visit!

Money-Saving Tips

Currency: Singapore Dollar (SGD or S$)


Singapore can be expensive, but there are many FREE things to do! Here's how to save money:


Free Attractions: Gardens by the Bay (outdoor), Merlion Park, Chinatown, Little India, walking along Marina Bay, and all beaches are FREE!


Cheap Food: Eat at hawker centres (food courts). Meals cost $3-5 instead of $15-30 at restaurants. Try Maxwell Food Centre, Lau Pa Sat, or Chinatown Complex.


Tourist Passes: Buy combination tickets for multiple attractions to save money. Use the Tourist Pass for unlimited MRT rides.

Sample Itineraries

1-Day Trip:

Morning: Visit Merlion Park and walk around Marina Bay
Lunch: Eat at a hawker centre
Afternoon: Gardens by the Bay
Evening: Watch the light show at Gardens by the Bay (7:45 PM or 8:45 PM)

3-Day Trip:

Day 1: Marina Bay area (Merlion, Marina Bay Sands, Gardens by the Bay)
Day 2: Sentosa Island (beaches and attractions)
Day 3: Cultural areas (Chinatown, Little India, Arab Street) + Singapore Zoo

5-Day Trip:

Add: Singapore Flyer, Orchard Road shopping, Night Safari, River Safari, Jurong Bird Park, and more food adventures!

Important Rules & Tips

Singapore has strict rules. Follow them or you might get fined!

❌ Common Prohibitions

  • No chewing gum (it's illegal to bring it in!)
  • No smoking except in designated areas
  • No littering (fine: $300–1000)
  • No jaywalking (cross at traffic lights only)
  • No eating/drinking on MRT (fine: $500)

✅ Useful Tips

  • Tap water is safe to drink
  • Tipping is not expected
  • English is widely spoken
  • Very safe city — you can walk around at night
  • Free Wi-Fi available at many places (Wireless@SGx)
